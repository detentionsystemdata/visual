Ways to see the detention system.

The same public registry, read many ways — county maps, a schematic state grid, who runs which territory, and a lens on the handful of facilities that hold almost everyone. Every panel names its source and date. Nothing here is editorial; pick the views worth building for real.

Who runs it · coverage

04Territory · field offices

Which ICE field office covers where

ICE splits the country into 25 ERO field-office areas of responsibility. Every county is shaded by the office that runs it. Pick one office to isolate its territory and see how many facilities sit inside.

or click a region on the map
scroll to load map…

Reading it: 25 offices can't each get a unique color, so hues repeat — hover names the office, and the selector isolates one at a time. Territory outlines come from ICE's county-to-office assignment; a facility's office is its own field, so the two can be cross-checked.

05Point map · courts

Courts, and their tie to detention

Courts and detention are run by different agencies (DOJ vs. ICE), but they connect on the ground: of these courts sit inside or beside a detention facility — where detained people's cases are heard, often by video. Red marks those; grey are community courts.

scroll to load map…

The link: the registry ties facilities to a co-located court by shared ZIP — its own derivation, labeled as such. Beyond co-location, every court also falls inside one field-office territory (panel 04). A per-case facility→court routing isn't in public data, so we don't assert it.

The human scale

06Scale · scatter

Detained people, next to the towns around them

Each dot is a county with a facility: how many people live there (across) against how many are detained there (up). Diagonal lines mark detention as a share of residents. In a few small counties, the detained population rivals everyone who lives there.

scroll to load…
Where detention looms largestdetained ÷ county residents

Reading it: residents are 2023 Census county population; detained is average daily population summed over a county's facilities. County is the reliable unit (facilities carry a county); a town-level version is possible where a facility sits in a named place.

07Concentration · interactive

A few facilities hold almost everyone

People worry about places like Adelanto, Dilley, El Paso, Stewart — the big dedicated centers. The data bears the instinct out. Drag the line: the top facilities by population account for the share shown. The long tail is county jails, hold rooms, and hospitals that report no standing population at all.
